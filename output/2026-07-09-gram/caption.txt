Scientists built an off-switch for an AI's most dangerous knowledge 🧠🔒

The dual-use problem: the same AI knowledge that helps cure a disease can help make one. Researchers at AE Studio and Anthropic introduced GRAM — during training, each dangerous skill (virology, cyber, nuclear) is packed into its own small, removable module. Delete the module and the model acts like it never learned the subject, keeping its normal abilities almost perfectly (~0.96) while the removed capability drops away — cleaner than data filtering or after-the-fact unlearning, and harder to retrain back. Tested up to 5B parameters; separation improves with scale. One training run, ~5× cheaper than training separate models.

Source: AE Studio & Anthropic — "Modular Pretraining Enables Access Control" (GRAM). Charts: AE Studio.
