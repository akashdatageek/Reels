OpenAI's first hardware is a $230 keyboard — the Codex Micro, built to control its AI coder. Launched July 15, already sold out. ⌨️

AI Receipts · Ep. 2

Co-designed with keyboard maker Work Louder, sold on OpenAI's own Supply Co. store: a dial that sets how hard the AI thinks, six keys that glow with each AI job's live status, a joystick that starts work, and keys to approve or reject what it built. Limited run — and no, it's not the Jony Ive device (that reported ~$6.5B project is still unreleased).
