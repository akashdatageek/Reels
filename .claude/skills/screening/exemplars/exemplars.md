# Screening exemplars — the taste bar

Every rubric judgment is comparative: "compared to these frames, does the
frame under review hold up?" Entries are stills that live in this folder.
Bars are what we aim at; anti-patterns are what a rejection taught us.
When the user rejects a shipped reel for a visual reason, the offending
still gets copied here as an anti-pattern AND the reason becomes a new
numbered rubric item in SKILL.md — rejections make the gate stricter.

All bars below are from the neutrality reel (2026-07-12), our reference
cut — marked **current internal bar**; replace them when a better frame
ships, don't let the bar rot.

## Bars

### scene_00_001.84.jpg — hook card · current internal bar
Headline lands fully-formed by 0.25s, wraps cleanly inside the margins, and
the one accent word ("neutral", "GROK") is the claim itself, not decoration.
Nothing on the frame competes with the sentence — this is what R1 means by
"the hook lands visually."

### scene_03_019.40.jpg — stat / sendable frame · current internal bar
97/108 at 220px is readable from across a room; the label under it carries
unit, population, and source in one line, so the frame survives being
screenshotted out of context (R4). The fraction bar repeats the number
visually — a viewer who reads nothing still gets "almost all."

### scene_06_045.57.jpg — figure payoff crop · current internal bar
The crop is tight enough that the payoff (Grok dot, "MOST NEUTRAL", the
center line) is legible on a 270px tile — R2's squint test. Our circleDraw
+ label sit on top of the source's own pixels; the evidence is the source's,
untouched, and the camera did the pointing.

### scene_04_024.17.jpg — figure overview (pre-zoom) · current internal bar
The wide view exists to orient, not to be read: the card shows the whole
chart for under 2s before the camera moves in. This is the acceptable use
of an unreadable-at-tile-size frame — it must be FOLLOWED by a tight crop
(this scene's focus step). A wide view that is the scene's ONLY framing
fails R2.
